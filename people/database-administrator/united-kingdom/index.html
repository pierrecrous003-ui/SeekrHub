What Does a Database Administrator Do?

A Database Administrator is responsible for applying professional expertise to support organisational goals. This typically involves analysis, planning, collaboration with stakeholders, and the use of specialised tools or methodologies relevant to the role.

In United Kingdom, Database Administrators often work alongside multidisciplinary teams to improve operational performance, solve complex problems, and support long-term strategic initiatives.
