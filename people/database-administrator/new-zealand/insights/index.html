Where Database Administrators Fit Within Organisations in New Zealand

Insights into the Database Administrator role in New Zealand

Database Administrators play an important role in how organisations operate and make decisions. In New Zealand, professionals in this role work across a variety of industries and organisational environments.

This page provides an overview of how Database Administrators typically function in New Zealand, including the skills they apply and the contexts in which they work.

Typical Responsibilities

  • Applying role-specific expertise to organisational challenges
  • Collaborating with teams and stakeholders
  • Supporting operational or strategic objectives
  • Adapting responsibilities based on organisational needs

What Does a Database Administrator Do?

A Database Administrator applies specialised knowledge to support organisational objectives. While responsibilities vary, the role commonly involves analysis, collaboration, and domain-specific problem solving.

Industries Where Database Administrators Work

  • Technology and digital services
  • Finance and professional services
  • Healthcare and life sciences
  • Manufacturing and operations
  • Public sector and government
